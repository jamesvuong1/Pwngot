Toasty Tomato Soup

By Geronimo Wilson | 1.5 hours

Description

Roasted, seasoned, and pureed tomatoes infused with smoked paprika and garlic chives. A rich, velvety soup perfect for cozy meals—serve with cream and fresh basil for extra taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ripe tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups vegetable broth (or chicken broth)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped garlic chives
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (optional, for serving)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Roast: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the tomatoes, onion, and garlic on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, season with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Roast for 30–35 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. Puree the Vegetables: Once roasted, transfer the vegetables to a blender or use an immersion blender to puree until smooth. Add vegetable broth gradually to reach your desired soup consistency.
  3. Heat the Soup: Pour the pureed soup into a pot and bring it to a simmer over medium heat. Adjust salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and swirl in heavy cream (optional). Garnish with fresh garlic chives and basil leaves.

Pro Tips

  • For an extra smoky flavor, roast the tomatoes a bit longer until they get a slightly charred edge.
  • Blend the soup with a bit of fresh basil for an herby touch.
  • Serve with grilled cheese sandwiches for the ultimate comfort meal.

Pumpkin Pie

By Jessica Monroe | 1.5 hours

Description

A classic dessert with smooth and creamy pumpkin filling encased in a buttery, flaky crust. Perfect for any time of year.

Ingredients

  • 1 (9-inch) pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can (15 oz) pure pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream (optional, for topping)

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prepare Crust: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and crimp the edges. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment, and bake for an additional 5 minutes to crisp the bottom. Set aside to cool.
  2. Mix Filling: In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, sugar, spices (cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves), salt, eggs, heavy cream, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Fill the Crust: Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared pie crust.
  4. Bake the Pie: Bake at 425°F for 15 minutes. Then, reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 40–45 minutes, or until the filling is set and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  5. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing. Top with whipped cream before serving, if desired.

Pro Tips

  • For a flakier crust, chill the pie crust dough before rolling it out.
  • Ensure the filling is well-mixed to avoid any clumps or lumps in the pumpkin mixture.
  • To add extra flavor, sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon on top of the whipped cream before serving.

Slow-Cooked Beef Stew

By Nelson Uganda | 2 hours

Description

A comforting, hearty dish with tender beef, carrots, and potatoes simmered in a rich, savory broth. Perfect for cold days, this stew can be customized with additional vegetables like peas, celery, or parsnips for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • 1 cup celery, chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup parsnips, peeled and sliced (optional)

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Brown the beef stew meat in batches, ensuring it gets a nice sear on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same skillet, sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Combine in Slow Cooker: Transfer the browned beef, sautéed onions, garlic, carrots, potatoes, and any optional vegetables into a slow cooker.
  4. Add Broth and Seasonings: Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Add thyme, rosemary, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  5. Slow Cook: Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  6. Add Peas: In the last 30 minutes of cooking, add the frozen peas (if using) and stir to combine.
  7. Serve: Once the stew is ready, remove bay leaves and serve hot with crusty bread or over rice.

Pro Tips

  • For a thicker stew, mash a few potatoes or carrots and stir them into the broth before serving.
  • If you're short on time, you can use a pressure cooker to speed up the process while maintaining tenderness.
  • Let the stew rest for 15-20 minutes before serving; this helps the flavors meld together more effectively.

Cheesy Jalapeno Poppers

By Tina Gomez | 45 mins

Description

A delicious appetizer made with fresh jalapenos stuffed with a creamy, cheesy filling, then wrapped in bacon and baked to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 12 fresh jalapenos
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 12 slices bacon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Jalapenos: Slice the jalapenos in half lengthwise and remove the seeds and membranes (wear gloves to avoid irritation). Set aside.
  2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, mix the softened cream cheese, shredded cheddar cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth and well combined.
  3. Stuff the Jalapenos: Spoon the cheesy filling into each jalapeno half, pressing gently to fill them completely.
  4. Wrap with Bacon: Cut each bacon slice in half and wrap one piece around each stuffed jalapeno. Secure with toothpicks if necessary.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the wrapped jalapenos on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and the cheese is bubbly.
  6. Serve: Remove from the oven, let cool for a few minutes, then serve warm as a delicious appetizer!

Pro Tips

  • For an extra spicy kick, leave some seeds in the jalapenos when removing the membranes.
  • If you want a smoky flavor, try adding some chipotle chili powder to the cheese mixture.
  • Serve with a cooling dip like ranch or sour cream to balance the heat from the jalapenos.

Spicy Mango Avocado Tacos

By Hernando Cucho De Stefano | 45 mins

Description

Spicy Mango Avocado Tacos are a fresh, vibrant dish with sweet mango, creamy avocado, crunchy veggies, and a zesty lime dressing. Perfect for any meal, these tacos offer a delightful balance of flavors and textures that are sure to impress your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage or lettuce
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey or agave syrup
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 small corn or flour tortillas

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Mango and Avocado: In a large bowl, combine the diced mango, diced avocado, red onion, and jalapeño (if using). Gently toss to combine.
  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lime juice, honey (or agave), salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Toss the Ingredients: Pour the dressing over the mango and avocado mixture. Gently toss until everything is well coated and combined.
  4. Warm the Tortillas: Heat the tortillas on a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side, until warm and soft.
  5. Assemble the Tacos: Spoon the mango-avocado mixture onto each tortilla. Top with shredded cabbage or lettuce and sprinkle with fresh cilantro.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately and enjoy your fresh, vibrant tacos!

Pro Tips

  • For extra crunch, add some toasted pumpkin seeds or crushed tortilla chips on top.
  • If you prefer a spicier taco, add a drizzle of hot sauce or some extra jalapeños.
  • For a more filling meal, consider adding grilled shrimp or chicken as a protein option.

Smoky Savory Paella

By Pranav Anand | 1.5 hours

Description

A vibrant, flavorful dish featuring tender rice, savory seafood, smoky chorizo, and aromatic saffron. Perfect for sharing with friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) short-grain rice (like Bomba or Arborio)
  • 3 1/2 cups (830ml) chicken or seafood stock (warm)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads (steeped in 2 tablespoons warm water)
  • 8 oz (225g) chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz (225g) chorizo, sliced
  • 12 large shrimp (shell on preferred for flavor)
  • 12 mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Base: Heat olive oil in a large paella pan or wide skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onions, garlic, and peppers until softened, about 5–7 minutes.
  2. Cook the Chicken and Chorizo: Add the chicken pieces and chorizo slices to the pan. Cook until the chicken is browned on all sides, about 7–8 minutes.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Rice: Stir in the diced tomatoes and smoked paprika. Cook for 2–3 minutes, then add the rice and coat it well with the oils and juices in the pan.
  4. Add Broth and Saffron: Pour in the warm stock and saffron mixture. Season lightly with salt and pepper, then stir gently to distribute the rice evenly. Do not stir again to form a proper paella crust (socarrat).
  5. Cook the Paella: Let the mixture simmer gently for 20–25 minutes, until the rice is almost cooked and most of the liquid is absorbed. Adjust heat to avoid burning.
  6. Add Seafood and Peas: Nestle the shrimp and mussels into the rice. Sprinkle the peas over the top and cover loosely with foil. Cook for an additional 10 minutes, until the seafood is cooked and the mussels have opened.
  7. Finish and Serve: Remove from heat. Let sit uncovered for 5 minutes. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

Pro Tips

  • For the best flavor, use shell-on shrimp and mussels as they release more taste into the broth.
  • If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can add a touch more smoked paprika or even a bit of smoked salt.
  • Let the paella sit for a few minutes off the heat before serving. This helps the rice absorb any remaining liquid and enhances the flavor.

Meaty Eggplant Moussaka

By Abdul Dell | 2.5 hours

Description

A rich and comforting Mediterranean classic with layers of roasted eggplant, savory spiced meat sauce, and a creamy béchamel topping.

Ingredients

  • For the Eggplant:
  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • Olive oil, for brushing
  • Salt
  • For the Meat Sauce:
  • 1 lb (450g) ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional, but recommended)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For the Béchamel Sauce:
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups milk, warmed
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For Assembly:
  • Additional Parmesan cheese, for topping

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Eggplant: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Lightly salt the eggplant slices and let sit for 20–30 minutes to draw out moisture. Pat dry with paper towels. Brush both sides with olive oil and roast on a baking sheet for about 20 minutes until tender and lightly golden. Set aside.
  2. Make the Meat Sauce: In a large skillet over medium heat, sauté the onion and garlic until soft. Add ground lamb or beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat if needed. Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, wine, cinnamon, allspice, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes until thickened. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Béchamel Sauce: Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 1–2 minutes (do not brown). Gradually whisk in warm milk while stirring constantly. Cook until thickened, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in nutmeg, Parmesan, salt, and pepper. Let cool slightly, then whisk in the beaten egg.
  4. Assemble the Moussaka: In a greased 9x13 inch baking dish, layer half the eggplant slices. Spread the meat sauce evenly over them, then top with remaining eggplant. Pour béchamel sauce over the top and sprinkle with extra Parmesan.
  5. Bake: Reduce oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 45–60 minutes, until top is golden and bubbling.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the moussaka rest for 20–30 minutes before slicing. Serve warm with salad or crusty bread.

Pro Tips

  • Salting the eggplant before roasting removes bitterness and improves texture.
  • Letting the béchamel cool slightly before adding the egg prevents it from scrambling.
  • Moussaka is even better the next day—reheat gently for deeper flavor and easier slicing.

Crispy Chicken Tenders

By James Smith | 25 mins

Description

Golden-brown chicken tenders with a crunchy exterior and juicy interior. Perfect with your favorite dipping sauce!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast fillets (cut into strips)
  • ½ cup flour (for dredging)
  • 2 large eggs (beaten)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko for extra crunch)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying (preferably canola oil)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken: Cut chicken breast into even strips and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Set up dredging stations: Place flour in first bowl, beaten eggs in second, and breadcrumbs mixed with seasonings in third.
  3. Bread the chicken: Coat each strip in flour, then egg, then breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
  4. Heat oil: Pour ½ inch of oil in skillet and heat to 350°F (medium-high heat).
  5. Fry the tenders: Cook in batches for 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown and internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Drain and serve: Transfer to paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Serve immediately with dipping sauces.

Tips

  • For extra crispiness, double coat by repeating the egg and breadcrumb steps
  • Try adding ¼ cup grated parmesan to the breadcrumbs for extra flavor
  • Bake at 400°F for 15-20 minutes for a healthier version

Chocolate Mug Cake

By Geronimo Wilson | 5 mins

Description

Chocolate mug cake is rich, soft, and ready in minutes. Customize with chips, peanut butter, or whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: chocolate chips, whipped cream for topping

Instructions

  1. Combine dry ingredients: In a microwave-safe mug, mix 1/4 cup flour, 2 tbsp cocoa powder, 2 tbsp sugar, 1/4 tsp baking powder, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Add wet ingredients: Stir in 1/4 cup milk, 2 tbsp vegetable oil, and 1/4 tsp vanilla extract until smooth and lump-free.
  3. Optional: Fold in a few chocolate chips for extra richness.
  4. Microwave: Cook on high for 60–90 seconds. The cake should rise and look set but still slightly gooey in the center.
  5. Cool and serve: Let cool for a minute. Top with whipped cream, more chocolate chips, or your favorite toppings.

Baking Tips

  • Use a wide mug to avoid overflow during microwaving.
  • Microwave times vary—start with 60 seconds and add more only if needed.
  • Drop a few chocolate chips in the center before microwaving for a gooey core.

Honey Soy Glazed Salmon

By Jackson Huang | 20 mins

Description

Honey soy salmon is sweet, savory, quick, and pan-seared with a sticky glaze. Serve with rice or veggies.

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger (grated)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Make the glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, lemon juice, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Cook the salmon: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add salmon fillets skin-side down. Sear for 3–4 minutes until golden.
  3. Glaze and finish: Flip the salmon and pour in the glaze. Cook for another 3–4 minutes, spooning glaze over the top until it thickens slightly and the salmon is cooked through.
  4. Serve: Garnish with sesame seeds if desired and serve with rice or steamed vegetables.

Pro Tips

  • Use skin-on salmon for easier flipping and added flavor.
  • Let the glaze simmer after adding it to enhance the caramelization.
  • Marinate the salmon for 15 minutes beforehand for deeper flavor.

Speedy Vegetable Stir Fry

By Chen Wei | 20 mins

Description

Colorful veggies in a savory garlic-ginger sauce, ready in minutes. Serve over rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prep vegetables: Wash and cut all vegetables into uniform sizes for even cooking.
  2. Heat wok: Heat oil in wok or large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  3. Aromatics: Add garlic and ginger, stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Cook hard vegetables: Add carrots and broccoli first, stir-fry for 2 minutes.
  5. Add remaining veggies: Add bell pepper and snap peas, stir-fry for another 3 minutes.
  6. Add sauces: Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil, tossing to coat evenly.
  7. Thicken sauce: Add cornstarch mixture and stir until sauce thickens and coats vegetables.
  8. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve immediately over rice or noodles.

Pro Tips

  • Have all ingredients prepped before starting as stir-fry cooks quickly
  • Keep the heat high to prevent vegetables from becoming soggy
  • Add protein like tofu, chicken, or shrimp for a complete meal

Korean Kimchi Fried Rice

By Archie Harper | 20 minutes

Description

Korean Kimchi Fried Rice is bold, spicy, and savory, made with fermented kimchi, steamed rice, vegetables, and a touch of sesame oil for deep, satisfying flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice)
  • 1 cup well-fermented kimchi, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons kimchi juice (optional but adds extra flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 green onions, finely sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg (optional, for topping)
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Nori (seaweed) strips, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Chop the kimchi and green onions, and have all other ingredients ready. If using cold rice, gently break it up with a spoon so it’s easy to stir-fry.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the garlic and white parts of the green onions, cooking for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Kimchi: Add the chopped kimchi to the pan and stir-fry for about 3–4 minutes until it softens and smells savory.
  4. Flavor and Fry: Stir in the gochujang and soy sauce, mixing everything well. Then add the rice and kimchi juice. Stir-fry, pressing the rice down slightly and flipping it to mix, until everything is hot and coated evenly with sauce (about 5–7 minutes).
  5. Finish Up: Drizzle the sesame oil over the fried rice. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. Top with an Egg (Optional): In a separate small pan, fry an egg sunny-side up. Top the kimchi fried rice with the egg, sprinkle with sesame seeds, and garnish with green onion tops or nori strips if desired.

Tips

  • Use day-old rice for the best texture—fresh rice can turn mushy.
  • Add diced Spam or bacon for a savory protein boost.
  • Want it spicier? Add a dash of Korean chili flakes (gochugaru).

Italian Spaghetti Carbonara

By Nelson Uganda | 25 minutes

Description

Italian Spaghetti Carbonara is creamy, savory, and comforting, made with eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, crispy pancetta, and black pepper.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Boil the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. Cook the Pancetta: While the pasta cooks, heat a skillet over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. Mix the Sauce: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Combine Everything: Quickly add the drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to coat in fat. Off heat, pour in the egg mixture and toss vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Use reserved pasta water to loosen as needed.
  5. Serve: Plate immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.

Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs to help the sauce emulsify better.
  • Work quickly off heat when adding the egg mixture to avoid scrambling.
  • Guanciale is traditional, but pancetta or even thick-cut bacon can be used.

Buffalo Chicken Wraps

By Geronimo Wilson | 25 minutes

Description

Buffalo Chicken Wraps are spicy, tangy, and satisfying, made with crispy or grilled chicken tossed in buffalo sauce, wrapped in a soft tortilla with fresh lettuce, creamy ranch or blue cheese dressing, and shredded cheese for a bold and easy meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken (grilled, rotisserie, or breaded tenders)
  • 1/3 cup buffalo sauce (like Frank’s RedHot)
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup ranch or blue cheese dressing
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced red onions (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (if reheating chicken)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken: If using pre-cooked chicken, reheat it in a skillet over medium heat with a little butter until warmed through. If cooking fresh, grill or pan-fry chicken breast strips until golden and cooked through.
  2. Toss in Buffalo Sauce: Place the warm chicken into a bowl and toss with buffalo sauce until evenly coated.
  3. Assemble the Wraps: Lay out tortillas. Layer lettuce, buffalo chicken, shredded cheese, and tomatoes or onions (if using). Drizzle with ranch or blue cheese dressing.
  4. Wrap It Up: Fold in the sides and roll each tortilla tightly.
  5. Optional: Toast wraps in a dry skillet for 1–2 minutes per side for a crispy finish.
  6. Serve: Slice in half and serve with extra dressing or celery sticks if desired.

Pro Tips

  • Use day-old grilled chicken for extra flavor and texture.
  • Warm tortillas slightly to make wrapping easier and prevent tearing.
  • Substitute blue cheese crumbles for an extra punch of flavor.
